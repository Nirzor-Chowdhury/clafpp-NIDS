# CLAF++ Deliverable

## Mathematical formulation
For a sample x, define:
- RE(x): reconstruction anomaly from Autoencoder plus GANomaly
- T(x): temporal anomaly from LSTM
- B(x): tabular discrimination score from RF/XGBoost
- P(x): feature-pattern score from CNN
- S_sym(x): symbolic confidence from neuro-symbolic rules

The CLAF++ score is:

S(x) = alpha(x) RE(x) + beta(x) T(x) + gamma(x) B(x) + delta(x) P(x) + lambda(x) S_sym(x)

All components are robustly normalized from validation-time normal traffic. Base weights are derived from validation AUPRC, and sample-specific weights are computed by a temperature-controlled softmax attention over normalized component intensities.

## Novelty claim
CLAF++ is novel because it combines distributional anomaly sensing, temporal modeling, tabular decision boundaries, local feature-pattern extraction, and symbolic priors in a single adaptive fusion layer. Relative to standalone GANomaly or Autoencoders, the system is more robust to temporal attacks, mixed attack families, and edge cases that benefit from explicit domain logic.

## Suggested paper title
CLAF++: Conditional Latent Anomaly Fusion with Ensemble Intelligence and Neuro-Symbolic Reasoning for Network Intrusion Detection

## Suggested abstract
We propose CLAF++, a hybrid network intrusion detection framework that integrates unsupervised generative anomaly modeling, temporal sequence learning, tabular ensemble classification, convolutional feature-pattern extraction, and neuro-symbolic reasoning in a unified decision layer. CLAF++ combines an Autoencoder and GANomaly to characterize manifold deviation, an LSTM to capture temporal attack dynamics, Random Forest and XGBoost to exploit tabular structure, and a 1D CNN to detect local traffic-feature motifs. A symbolic rule engine injects interpretable expert knowledge for behaviors such as scan activity and authentication abuse. The final anomaly score is produced by an adaptive fusion mechanism that weights each component according to validation-derived usefulness and per-sample anomaly intensity. To avoid leakage, the stacking-based meta learner is trained only on validation predictions generated by frozen base models. This design yields a reproducible, explainable, and publication-oriented IDS pipeline that improves comparability against generative, temporal, and classical baselines.
